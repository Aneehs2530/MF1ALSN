EMPOWERING LIFELONG LEARNERS

ALS Offers

ALS provides a second chance for individuals who were unable to complete their formal education.

Alternative Learning System:

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is a parallel education program in the Philippines designed for out-of-school youth and adults (OSYA). It offers non-formal and informal learning to help them gain basic literacy and complete basic education, providing flexible options suited to their needs. Since 2016, ALS has undergone major reforms to improve quality, access, and relevance. Key developments include: Enhanced ALS K to 12 Curriculum ALS Program 2.0 and a 5-Year Strategic Roadmap Monitoring & Evaluation System Handbook (2022) ALS ICT Strategic Plan and Research Agenda (2022-2026) The ALS Report 2023 documents progress and outlines recommendations for building a stronger, more inclusive second-chance education system, as envisioned in the 1986 Philippine Constitution. The ALS K to 12 Curriculum is contextualized and aligned (but not identical) to the formal system, covering six learning strands: Communication Skills (English & Filipino) Scientific and Critical Thinking Mathematical and Problem-Solving Skills Life and Career Skills Understanding the Self and Society Digital Citizenship ALS empowers OSYA learners to continue learning, improve their lives, and contribute to society.

ALS K to 12 Basic Education Curriculum Learning Strands:

Learning Strands Skills Learning Strand 1 Communication Skills (English) Communication Skills (Filipino) Learning Strand 2 Scientific Literacy and Critical Thinking Learning Strand 3 Mathematical and Problem Solving Skills Learning Strand 4 Life and Career Skills Learning Strand 5 Understanding the Self and Society Learning Strand 6 Digital Citizenship

Community-Based Learning:

ALS often emphasizes community-based learning, promoting self-help and community development.
